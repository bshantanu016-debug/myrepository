## Post 2

**10 april ago**

**Content**:When a publisher elevates traffic to its primary KPI, it changes how the newsroom thinks about its output. This can lead down a very dangerous path.

In my latest newsletter, I provide a cautionary tale about a publisher that went too far in the chase after Google clicks and ends up in a self-dug hole with no easy way out.

I wish it was a fictional scenario, but it's all too real. I've seen it play out dozens and dozens of times at publishers of all sizes and scopes. When a newsroom elevates traffic to its core KPI, things change - and not for the better.

The danger of the chase is that it works. You get more clicks, more traffic, more revenue.

Until Google shuts the door.

Can you recover? Can you get that traffic back? Maybe, to an extent. If you're willing to be honest with yourself, and are willing to make painful changes.
